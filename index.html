Hi there! My name is Sazen Shakya, and I'm a first-year computer science major at the University of California Riverside. Originally from Colorado, I grew up in San Diego, California, and I'm passionate about becoming a software developer once I complete my degree.

Throughout my journey in learning C++ and Java, I have undertaken several coding projects to enhance my skills. One of my earliest projects involved recreating a famous old game called Lunar Lander using Java. It was an exciting experience as I delved into game development and learned how to implement various game mechanics.

Another notable project I worked on was during a Hack-a-thon hosted by UCR. It was an intense 12-hour coding session where my group and I had to develop a project from the initial idea to its completion. We decided to create an energy map that analyzed a large dataset with tens of thousands of entries. The program would then generate a graph showcasing the energy usage in the United States over time and compare it to the average growth during that period. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience to work collaboratively under time constraints and deliver a functional project.
